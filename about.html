About This Operation

Who's behind this? A loose confederation of extremely annoyed Doland residents who got tired of being eaten alive and decided standing water had it coming.

Who We Are

Not a nonprofit. Not a government program. Just spite with a domain name.
FILE 1 — ORIGIN

Operation: Doland Skeeter War started the way most good ideas do: someone got bit eleven times in one evening and decided enough was enough.

There's no 501(c)(3), no city budget line, and no official sanction. This is a neighbor-to-neighbor effort run by residents of Doland, South Dakota who were tired of losing every cookout to Culex tarsalis and figured the internet could help recruit reinforcements.

We are not affiliated with the City of Doland, Spink County, the State of South Dakota, or any agency that has to answer a public records request. If this were official, it would have a logo committee and a 40-page style guide. Instead it has a guy with a bucket and a grudge.

Why This Exists

Because nobody else was going to do it.
FILE 3 — RATIONALE

The town sprays the roads. The county and state run real, professional mosquito control programs — credit to them, that's legitimate work with a real budget. But none of that reaches the tire in your side yard, the clogged gutter, or the kiddie pool nobody's emptied since June. That part is legally, physically, and permanently your job: no truck, no crew, and no agency can roll onto your private property and drain it for you.

This site exists to close that gap — plain-language, no-budget, works-with-stuff-you-already-own guidance for the part of the fight that only you can run.

There is no upsell, no newsletter, and no app. Just a Bti dunk, a bucket, and a grudge — freely given.
